# HAE Strategic Analysis Manual
## Integrated Strategy Analysis Framework: Medical Affairs x Marketing x HEOR

---

## Overview

This manual serves as the Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) for conducting a reproducible, integrated strategic analysis across Medical Affairs, Marketing, and HEOR in the Hereditary Angioedema (HAE) therapeutic area.

**Deliverables (11 outputs):**

| # | Deliverable | Purpose |
|---|------------|---------|
| 1 | Executive Summary | High-level overview and decision-support brief |
| 2 | U.S. Strategy Flow | Strategic flow for the U.S. market |
| 3 | International Strategy (INTL) Flow | Strategic flow for international markets |
| 4 | U.S. vs INTL SWOT | Regional strengths and weaknesses comparison |
| 5 | Above-Market Integration | Global integration strategy |
| 6 | X1->X2->Y->Z Chain | Strategy -> Tactics -> Evidence -> Publication chain |
| 7 | Vulnerability Analysis | Risk and vulnerability assessment |
| 8 | KPIs | Key Performance Indicators |
| 9 | IEGP Strategic Materials | Integrated Evidence Generation Plan materials |
| 10 | FY27 HAE Summary | Annual summary |

## Phase 1: Disease Landscape Analysis (Day 1-3)

### Step 1.1: Disease Understanding Update

**Activities:**
- Review updated HAE Type I/II/III classification and latest patient prevalence estimates
- Pathophysiology of attacks (bradykinin pathway)
- Current treatment paradigm (prophylaxis vs on-demand)
- Quantification of unmet needs

**Data Sources:**
- PubMed/MEDLINE (systematic reviews from the past 3 years)
- ClinicalTrials.gov (ongoing trials)
- Patient registry data (e.g., Icatibant Outcome Survey)
- Congress presentations (ACAAI, EAACI, WAO)

**Output:** Disease landscape summary (1-2 pages)

### Step 1.2: Competitive Analysis

**Products in Scope:**

| Category | Product | Manufacturer | MoA |
|----------|---------|-------------|-----|
| Prophylaxis (mAb) | Takhzyro (lanadelumab) | Takeda | Anti-kallikrein |
| Prophylaxis (oral) | Orladeyo (berotralstat) | BioCryst | Kallikrein inhibitor |
| Prophylaxis (SC) | Haegarda (C1-INH SC) | CSL Behring | C1-INH replacement |
| Prophylaxis (novel) | Donidalorsen | Ionis/Otsuka | Prekallikrein ASO |
| Prophylaxis (novel) | Garadacimab | CSL Behring | Anti-Factor XIIa |
| On-demand | Firazyr (icatibant) | Takeda | B2 receptor antagonist |
| On-demand | Berinert (C1-INH IV) | CSL Behring | C1-INH replacement |

**Analytical Dimensions:**
- Efficacy (attack frequency reduction rate, proportion of attack-free patients)
- Safety (injection site reactions, hepatotoxicity, immunogenicity)
- Administration convenience (SC self-injection vs IV vs oral vs dosing frequency)
- Insurance coverage and access (Prior Authorization requirements, Step therapy)
- Pricing/cost (WAC, patient out-of-pocket, PPPM)
- Pipeline threats (novel MoAs in Phase 2/3)

**Output:** Competitive comparison matrix + Pipeline threat assessment

### Step 1.3: KOL/Stakeholder Mapping

**Activities:**
- Develop U.S. and international KOL (Key Opinion Leader) lists
- Rank by publication volume, clinical trial involvement, and congress presentations
- Assess KOL positions and potential biases
- Identify key payer/HTA decision-makers

**Data Sources:**
- PubMed author search
- ClinicalTrials.gov PI search
- Congress programs (ACAAI/EAACI/AAAAI)

**Output:** KOL map (Influence x Alignment matrix)

## Phase 2: Strategy Flow Design (Day 4-7)

### Step 2.1: U.S. Strategy Flow

**Framework:**

```
[Market Insight] -> [Strategic Imperative] -> [Brand Strategy] -> [Tactical Plan]

Market Insight:
  - Patient journey barriers
  - Prescriber decision drivers
  - Payer evaluation criteria

Strategic Imperative:
  - Positioning statement
  - Differentiation messaging
  - Target segmentation

Brand Strategy:
  - Stage-specific strategies across Awareness -> Trial -> Adoption -> Loyalty
  - Synergy design with Medical Affairs activities

Tactical Plan:
  - MSL engagement plan
  - Speaker Program
  - Advisory Board
  - Congress Strategy
```

**U.S.-Specific Considerations:**
- Medicare/Medicaid vs Commercial insurance
- Specialty Pharmacy distribution
- REMS (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) requirements
- Patient Support Program (Hub Services)

**Output:** U.S. Strategy Flow diagram (1 page)

### Step 2.2: International Strategy (INTL) Flow

**Apply the same framework to the following regions:**

| Region | Key Markets | Region-Specific Considerations |
|--------|------------|-------------------------------|
| EU | DE/FR/UK/IT/ES | EMA approval, NICE/G-BA/HAS assessments, reference pricing |
| Japan | JP | PMDA approval, NHI price listing, Chuikyo process |
| Asia-Pacific | KR/AU/TW | Country-specific approval and reimbursement processes |
| LATAM | BR/MX | Patient access programs |

**International-Specific Considerations:**
- HTA (Health Technology Assessment) submission strategy
- IRP (International Reference Pricing) implications
- Local evidence needs (RWE, epidemiological data)
- Launch timing optimization

**Output:** INTL Strategy Flow diagrams (by region, 2-3 pages)

### Step 2.3: U.S. vs INTL SWOT

**Conduct a regional comparison across four quadrants:**

| | U.S. | INTL |
|---|------|------|
| **Strengths** | Early approval, high price tolerance, large patient pool | Multi-market simultaneous launch, HTA leverage |
| **Weaknesses** | Payer barriers (PA/ST), high out-of-pocket costs | Approval delays, pricing pressure |
| **Opportunities** | RWE utilization, label expansion | Untapped markets, local KOL development |
| **Threats** | Biosimilars, novel oral agents | Reference pricing cascade |

**Analytical Dimensions:**
- Speed and depth of market access
- Regional variation in evidence gaps
- Regional competitive positioning
- Evolving regulatory environment

**Output:** U.S. vs INTL SWOT comparison table (1 page)

## Phase 3: Above-Market Integration Strategy (Day 8-10)

### Step 3.1: Above-Market Integration

**Objective:** Develop a global integration framework that spans regional strategies

**Elements to Integrate:**
- Global brand story (core messaging consistent across all regions)
- Global evidence strategy (which evidence assets serve which regions)
- Global KOL network (cross-regional KOL collaboration design)
- Launch sequence optimization (which markets to prioritize and why)

**Framework:**
```
Above-Market Strategy
  |-- Global Brand Positioning
  |-- Global Evidence Generation Plan
  |-- Global Medical Education Platform
  +-- Global Commercial Excellence
       |-- U.S. Execution
       |-- EU5 Execution
       |-- Japan Execution
       +-- RoW Execution
```

**Output:** Above-Market Integration diagram (1 page)

### Step 3.2: X1->X2->Y->Z Chain (Strategic Chain)

**The most critical framework. Visualize the logical chain connecting all activities:**

```
X1 (Strategy)           X2 (Tactics)              Y (Evidence)           Z (Publication)
-----------------       ------------------        -----------------      ------------------
Differentiated     ->   MSL Discussion Guide  ->  RWE Study Design   ->  Peer-reviewed paper
  positioning      ->   Advisory Board        ->  Expert Consensus   ->  Congress poster
                   ->   Speaker Training      ->  Case Series        ->  Review article

Securing access    ->   HEOR Model Development -> CEA/BIA Results    ->  HEOR publication
                   ->   Payer Advisory Board   -> Budget Impact Data ->  Value dossier

Label expansion    ->   Investigator Meeting   -> Phase 3b/4 Trial   ->  Full publication
                   ->   Registry Collaboration -> Registry Outcomes   ->  Registry report
```

**Design Rules:**
1. Every X2 must be logically derived from an X1
2. Every Y must represent evidence required to fulfill an X2
3. Every Z must be a publication or deliverable arising from a Y
4. Reverse traceability must be possible (Z -> Y -> X2 -> X1)

**Output:** X1->X2->Y->Z matrix (2-3 pages)

## Phase 5: KPI Design and Alignment (Day 14-16)

### Step 5.1: KPI Design

**Three-Tier KPI Structure:**

```
KGI (Key Goal Indicators) -- Ultimate outcome metrics
  |-- Market Share (NRx/TRx share)
  |-- Revenue
  +-- Patient Reach (treated patients)

KPI (Key Performance Indicators) -- Leading indicators
  |-- Medical Affairs KPIs
  |   |-- MSL Reach (unique HCP touchpoints)
  |   |-- Scientific Exchange Quality Score
  |   |-- Publication Output (IF-weighted)
  |   +-- Congress Engagement Metrics
  |-- Marketing KPIs
  |   |-- Brand Awareness (aided/unaided)
  |   |-- NRx Growth Rate
  |   |-- Digital Engagement
  |   +-- Campaign ROI
  +-- HEOR KPIs
      |-- HTA Submission Outcomes
      |-- Formulary Access Rate
      |-- PPPM Cost Position
      +-- RWE Study Completion Rate

KAI (Key Action Indicators) -- Activity metrics
  |-- # Advisory Boards conducted
  |-- # Speaker Programs
  |-- # MSL-HCP interactions
  +-- # HEOR models completed
```

### Step 5.2: KPI/KGI Alignment Diagram

**Causal map showing how each KPI contributes to KGIs:**

```
KAI (Activity)          KPI (Leading)            KGI (Outcome)
-------------------     -------------------      -------------------
MSL engagements    ->   HCP awareness      ->   NRx Share increase
Publication output ->   SOV (Share of Voice) ->  Revenue growth
HTA submissions    ->   Formulary Access   ->   Patient Reach increase
Advisory Boards    ->   Expert Alignment   ->   Market Share increase
```

**Output:** KPI/KGI Alignment Diagram (1 page)

## Phase 6: IEGP and Annual Summary (Day 17-20)

### Step 6.1: IEGP (Integrated Evidence Generation Plan) Strategic Materials

**IEGP Components:**
- Evidence Gap Analysis (identification of evidence gaps)
- Study Prioritization Matrix (priority ranking)
- Study Concept Sheets (summary for each study)
- Timeline and Milestones
- Budget Allocation
- Cross-functional Alignment

**Alignment Check with X1->X2->Y->Z Chain:**
Clearly map where each IEGP study sits within the X1->Z chain

### Step 6.2: FY27 HAE Summary

**Annual Summary Structure:**
1. Executive Dashboard (1 page)
2. Annual KPI performance (actuals vs targets)
3. Key activity highlights
4. Year-in-review competitive landscape
5. Implications and recommendations for the next fiscal year
6. Budget performance

### Step 6.3: Executive Summary (Final Integration)

**C-suite-ready summary integrating all deliverables:**
- Page 1: Market landscape, competitive position, key risks
- Page 2: Strategic direction (U.S. + INTL)
- Page 3: KPIs and return on investment
- Page 4: Next fiscal year action plan
